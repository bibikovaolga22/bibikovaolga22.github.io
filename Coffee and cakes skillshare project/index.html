PROBLEM OVERVIEW

The core idea of the current project was to create a mobile app that would help people to make their order quickly and efficiently. In order to be clear about what I want to do in my project in the very beginning I started with scoping the topic and identifying what actually I am going to make.

Build a mobile app for different people of different social groups (students, workers, retired people) who use delivery services for getting coffee(drinks) and sweets during their work and leisure time.

Also to determine my strategy of the design I used Double Dimond design model. This model serves as a map that helps me to organize my thoughts in order to improve the creative process. I included there necessary methods an deliverables to be used in each stage of the design procedure.

RESEARCH AND SYNTHESIS

The user research is a milestone of the user-centered design(UCD) approach. It's essential part of the design that enables to define user's needs, behavors and pain points and in consiquence allows to achieve better results of the final product performence.In order to to capture and explore the reaction and behaviors of the users and also to make clear the unknown I conducted qualitative research - 30 minutes of online semi-structured interview with 4 users involved. Basically the main outcome of the research was that users wish to get their order delivered to them as soon as possible. It was also important for the users they could see discriptive information about the products they wanted to buy.
You can see the full research script here. According to the data gathered during the research I reframed the insight statements as "HMW" questions, e.g "How might we help users to make their orders as quickly as possible" or "How might we provide users with descriptive information about the products they try to order " etc.

USER TESTING AND ITERATION

User -Testing is another importent part of the design process. This technique allowes to check how usable the interface is. It reduces the risks of building the wrong product, finds the problems on the early stage of the process when it's easy and cheap to fix them and also saves money and time.The user-testing pursued the following goals:
-Validate the design solution
-Ensure that the content makes sence to the user
-Validate that the workflow is usable.
The user-testing was conducted online where the users were asked to describe what they see on the screen and answer open-ended questions. Generally the participants were satisfied with the low-fidelity interface and the user-flow was completed without any interruptions, doubts or problems. The only remark, that the users mentioned in the end was 'the ascence of 'heart' icon in coffee menu'. I took it into account and made some amendments in the next itteration.You can see full user-testing script here.
